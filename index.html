Trig Lesson 2

Graphs of the Primary Trig Functions

Review the Unit Circle, CAST Rule, and Parent Sinusoidal Graphs.

1. The Unit Circle

The unit circle is a circle with a radius of $r = 1$ centered at the origin. Its equation is $x^2 + y^2 = 1$. By picking a point $(x, y)$ on the circle formed by an angle $\theta$ from the positive x-axis, we define the primary trig ratios:

Cosine

$\cos \theta = \frac{x}{r} = x$

Sine

$\sin \theta = \frac{y}{r} = y$

Tangent

$\tan \theta = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}$

2. The CAST Rule

The CAST Rule helps you quickly determine which primary trigonometric ratios are positive in each of the four quadrants on the Cartesian plane.

  • Quadrant I (A): All ratios are positive.
  • Quadrant II (S): Only Sine is positive.
  • Quadrant III (T): Only Tangent is positive.
  • Quadrant IV (C): Only Cosine is positive.

3. Parent Graphs

Memorizing the key points of the primary trig functions is essential before exploring transformations.

$y = \sin \theta$

  • Shape: Wave starting at the origin (0,0), heading upwards.
  • Key Points (0° to 360°): (0°, 0), (90°, 1), (180°, 0), (270°, -1), (360°, 0)
  • Period: 360°
  • Amplitude: 1
  • Max / Min: 1 / -1

$y = \cos \theta$

  • Shape: Wave starting at its maximum peak (0,1).
  • Key Points (0° to 360°): (0°, 1), (90°, 0), (180°, -1), (270°, 0), (360°, 1)
  • Period: 360°
  • Amplitude: 1
  • Max / Min: 1 / -1

$y = \tan \theta$

  • Shape: Series of upward curves separated by vertical asymptotes.
  • Key Features: Has vertical asymptotes wherever $\cos \theta = 0$ (e.g., 90°, 270°).
  • Period: 180°
  • Amplitude: None (Undefined, approaches infinity)
