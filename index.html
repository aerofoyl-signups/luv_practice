A periodic function \( f(x) \) has a period of 8. The values of \( f(1) \), \( f(5) \), and \( f(7) \) are -3, 2, and 8, respectively. Predict the value of each of the following:

Explanation: Since the period is 8, \( f(x) = f(x + 8n) \) for any integer \( n \).
a) \( f(9) = f(1 + 8) = f(1) = -3 \)
b) \( f(29) = f(5 + 24) = f(5 + 3 \times 8) = f(5) = 2 \)
c) \( f(63) = f(7 + 56) = f(7 + 7 \times 8) = f(7) = 8 \)

Explanation:

While visiting the east coast of Canada, Ranouf notices that the water level at a town dock changes during the day as the tides come in and go out. Markings show a high tide of 3.3 meters at 6:30 a.m., a low tide of 0.7 meters at 12:40 p.m., and a high tide again at 6:50 p.m.

m
Explanation:
a) The period is the time between two consecutive high tides: from 6:30 a.m. to 6:50 p.m. is exactly 12 hours and 20 minutes.
b) Amplitude is calculated as: \( \frac{\text{Max} - \text{Min}}{2} = \frac{3.3 - 0.7}{2} = \frac{2.6}{2} = 1.3 \) meters.
4

The people mover at an airport shuttles between the main terminal and a satellite terminal 300 meters away. A one-way trip, moving at a constant speed, takes 1 minute, and the car remains at each terminal for 30 seconds before leaving.

min
m
Explanation:
a) One complete cycle includes traveling there (1 min), waiting (0.5 min), traveling back (1 min), and waiting again (0.5 min). Total Period = 1 + 0.5 + 1 + 0.5 = 3 minutes.
b) The distance fluctuates between 0m and 300m. Amplitude = \( \frac{300 - 0}{2} = 150 \) meters.

The Bay of Fundy has the highest tides in the world. A graph of the tides shows a maximum water depth of 12 meters at 2:30 AST, and a minimum depth of 1 meter at 8:45 AST. The next maximum depth of 12 meters occurs at 15:00 AST.

h
m
Explanation:
a) Period is the time between consecutive maximums: 15:00 - 02:30 = 12.5 hours.
b) Amplitude = \( \frac{\text{Max} - \text{Min}}{2} = \frac{12 - 1}{2} = 5.5 \) m.
c) Equation of the axis = \( \frac{\text{Max} + \text{Min}}{2} = \frac{12 + 1}{2} = 6.5 \). So, \( y = 6.5 \).

A rider's height above the ground on a Ferris wheel is a periodic function of time. You start at the bottom, reaching a minimum height of 1 m at \( t = 0 \) s. You reach the very top at a maximum height of 7 m at \( t = 4 \) s, and return to the bottom at \( t = 8 \) s.

m
m

b) Diameter = Max Height - Min Height = 7 m - 1 m = 6 m.
c) Axis height = \( \frac{\text{Max} + \text{Min}}{2} = \frac{7 + 1}{2} = 4 \) m.

Recall the periodic function \( f(x) \) from Question 1 which has a period of 8. The known values are \( f(1) = -3 \), \( f(5) = 2 \), and \( f(7) = 8 \).

Explanation:
Because the period is 8, \( f(x) = f(x - 8) \).
Therefore, \( f(40) = f(32) = f(24) = f(16) = f(8) = f(0) \).
We only know the values for \( f(1), f(5), \) and \( f(7) \). Without knowing \( f(0) \) or \( f(8) \), the value cannot be predicted.

Recall Ranouf's dock from Question 3. A high tide occurs at 6:30 a.m., a low tide at 12:40 p.m., and the next high tide at 6:50 p.m. (The period is 12 hours and 20 minutes).

Explanation:
The period is 12 hours and 20 minutes.
The previous low tide was at 12:40 p.m.
12:40 p.m. + 12 hours = 12:40 a.m.
12:40 a.m. + 20 minutes = 1:00 a.m.
11

For the basic trigonometric function \( y = \sin x \), what is the amplitude of its graph?

Explanation: The graph of \( y = \sin x \) reaches a maximum of 1 and a minimum of -1. Amplitude = \( \frac{1 - (-1)}{2} = 1 \).

For the basic trigonometric function \( y = \cos x \), what is the period of its graph in degrees?

°
Explanation: The standard cosine curve completes one full cycle over \( 360^\circ \).
13

Unlike sine and cosine, the tangent function \( y = \tan x \) has a different repeating length. What is the period of \( y = \tan x \) in degrees?

°
Explanation: The tangent function repeats itself every \( 180^\circ \) (e.g., between asymptotes at -90° and 90°).
14

Using the CAST rule, determine the sign of the trigonometric ratio: \( \cos 135^\circ \)

Explanation: \( 135^\circ \) lies in Quadrant II. According to the CAST rule, only Sine (S) is positive in Quadrant II. Therefore, Cosine is negative.
15

Using the CAST rule, determine the sign of the trigonometric ratio: \( \sin(-150^\circ) \)

Explanation: Rotating backwards \( -150^\circ \) lands in Quadrant III (coterminal with \( 210^\circ \)). In Quadrant III, only Tangent (T) is positive. Therefore, Sine is negative.

Ray is swinging back and forth in front of a motion detector. The equation of the axis of his motion is \( y = 9 \) m, and the amplitude is 5 m.

m
Explanation: Minimum Value = Axis - Amplitude = \( 9 - 5 = 4 \) m.
17

An oscilloscope hooked up to an AC circuit shows a sine curve with a maximum current of 4.5 amperes and a minimum current of -4.5 amperes.

amperes
Explanation: Amplitude = \( \frac{\text{Max} - \text{Min}}{2} = \frac{4.5 - (-4.5)}{2} = \frac{9}{2} = 4.5 \) amperes.
18

The diameter of a car's tire is 52 cm. While driven, it picks up a nail. Modeling the height of the nail above the ground as a periodic function, the height will vary between 0 cm and 52 cm.

cm
Explanation: Max = 52 cm, Min = 0 cm. Amplitude = \( \frac{52 - 0}{2} = 26 \) cm.

Bueller rides a Ferris wheel. A graph of his height shows a maximum height of 22 m and a minimum height of 2 m.

m
Explanation: The diameter is the difference between the highest point and the lowest point on the wheel. Diameter = Max - Min = 22 m - 2 m = 20 m.
